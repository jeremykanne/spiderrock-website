Glossary of Common Terms

Definitions of key terms used in stock and options trading, market data, and options analytics.

A

Auction Data
Trade data from options trades that are routed to and matched in exchange-sponsored marketplaces outside the posted exchange market.

B

Box Trades
A combination of options positions that create a synthetic bond-like instrument by combining a bull call spread and a bear put spread on the same underlying security.
Butterfly Spread
An options strategy combining bull and bear spreads with the same expiration date, using three different strike prices. The strategy profits when the underlying stays near the middle strike at expiration.

C

Calendar Spread
An options strategy involving buying and selling options with the same strike price but different expiration dates. Also known as a horizontal spread, it profits from the difference in time decay between the two expirations.
Call Options
Contracts giving the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy an underlying asset at a specified price (the strike price) on or before a specific expiration date.
COB Data
Close of Business data; end-of-day snapshots of prices, positions, and market activity captured at the close of the trading session.
Collar
A protective options strategy combining a long put and short call on an existing stock position. The collar limits both downside risk and upside potential within a defined range.
CTA Feed
Consolidated Tape Association feed; provides real-time and historical trade and quote data for NYSE-listed and NYSE American securities. Part of the National Market System (NMS).

D

Delta
A measure of an option's price sensitivity relative to a $1 change in the underlying asset's price. Ranges from 0 to 1 for calls and -1 to 0 for puts. Also approximates the probability that an option will expire in the money.

E

EOD Options Data
End-of-day options data capturing closing prices, implied volatilities, Greeks, and volumes at the close of each trading session. Foundational dataset for backtesting and historical volatility research.

G

Gamma
The rate of change of an option's delta for a $1 change in the underlying price. Measures the curvature of an option's price relative to the underlying. High gamma means delta changes rapidly with underlying moves.

H

Historical Option Analytics
Analytics derived from historical options data including implied volatility, Greeks, and surface data. Used for backtesting trading strategies, calibrating pricing models, and conducting quantitative research.

I

Implied Volatility
The market's forecast of the likely magnitude of movement in a security's price, derived from the market price of an options contract. Unlike historical (realized) volatility, implied volatility is forward-looking and reflects market consensus on expected risk.

M

Market Data API
A programmatic interface for accessing real-time or historical market data including prices, volumes, and analytics. Enables integration of market data into trading systems, risk platforms, and analytical tools via WebSocket or REST protocols.
Multicast Market Data Feeds
A one-to-many data delivery mechanism where a single data stream is sent simultaneously to multiple recipients over a network. Highly efficient for distributing high-frequency market data to many consumers with minimal latency overhead.

N

Normalized OPRA Feed
A processed version of the OPRA feed that reduces bandwidth to approximately 1/10th of the raw feed while preserving critical price and quote information. SpiderRock's normalization focuses on NBBO price level changes while holding back size-only updates.

O

OPRA Data
Options Price Reporting Authority data; the consolidated options market data feed for all US listed options. OPRA aggregates quote and trade information from all US options exchanges and distributes it via a single multicast stream.
Option Greeks
Measures of option price sensitivity to various factors: Delta (underlying price), Gamma (delta change), Theta (time decay), Vega (implied volatility), Rho (interest rates), and Phi (dividend rate).
Option Skew
The difference in implied volatility between out-of-the-money and at-the-money options for the same expiration. Skew reflects supply and demand imbalances and tail risk premiums — typically higher for downside puts than upside calls in equity markets.
Options Analytics
Quantitative metrics derived from options market data including implied volatility, Greeks, volatility surfaces, and trade analytics. SpiderRock's options analytics are computed on every option tick using proprietary numerical pricing libraries.

P

Put Options
Contracts giving the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell an underlying asset at a specified price (the strike price) on or before a specific expiration date. Used for downside protection or directional bearish bets.

R

Real Time Analytics
Analytics computed and delivered in real time as market conditions change, including implied volatility and Greeks updated on every option tick. SpiderRock's real-time analytics update within sub-millisecond timeframes for all US-listed options.
Rho
A measure of an option's sensitivity to changes in interest rates. Rho is typically most significant for long-dated options and LEAPS where the cost of carrying the position is affected more by interest rate changes.

T

Theta
A measure of the rate of decline in an option's value due to the passage of time, also known as time decay. Theta accelerates as expiration approaches and is typically negative for long options positions.

V

Vega
A measure of an option's sensitivity to changes in implied volatility. A vega of 0.10 means the option's value changes by $0.10 for each 1% change in implied volatility.
Vertical Spread
An options strategy using two options of the same type (both calls or both puts) with the same expiration but different strike prices. Provides defined risk and reward with reduced cost compared to outright options positions.
Volatility Surface
A three-dimensional representation of implied volatility across different strike prices and expiration dates for a given underlying asset. SpiderRock's volatility surfaces are computed continuously and are designed to be arbitrage-free across all strikes.
