Overview

What Are Volatility Surfaces?

Theoretical volatility surfaces are models of the fair market volatility of an option. Volatility is a measure of the speed and amount of change in the underlying asset. For options, it's a vital part of how they are priced and valued.

The surface for an option is the fit of the market between the bid and ask for a given term. SpiderRock operates servers that continually compute implied volatility surfaces for all option expiration months with live market quotes, archiving these surfaces at regular intervals for all options on stocks, ETFs, and options on futures indexes.

SpiderRock surfaces reflect a best fit of current markets and are subject to change if or when market conditions change. These surfaces are proven in live market operations and provide more accurate valuations than simple surface fits like averaging prices or taking the bid/ask as closing price.

About Our Feed

Options Price Feed with Volatility Surfaces

SpiderRock continually computes dynamic implied volatility surfaces for all options expiration months, updating as new live market quotes are received. Each curve record contains:

  • •Spline parameters for the shape of a surface
  • •Live calibrated at-the-money volatility
  • •Stock dividend rate and price offset
  • •Other related curve adjustments

Strikes on the X-axis expressed in moneyness (lognormal or normal), volatilities on Y-axis as a multiple of ATM volatility.

Surface Types

Three Volatility Surface Datasets

Surface Curve

Spline curve parameters, implied volatility surfaces, Greeks, and hedge deltas for all options expiration months. Two-dimensional curve with strikes in moneyness on the X-axis and volatilities as a multiple of ATM vol on the Y-axis.

Surface Grid Records (Skew)

Fixed grid of volatility for a given expiration as a function of delta for uncensored curves. Includes days until the next earnings date and implied earnings moves — ideal for skew analysis and tail risk modeling.

Fixed Term Surface (Censored)

Snapshots of censored ATM volatility term structure with the impact of implied earnings events removed. Constant maturity points: 5d, 10d, 21d, 42d, 63d, 84d, 105d, 126d, 189d, 252d, 378d, and 504d (2 years).
